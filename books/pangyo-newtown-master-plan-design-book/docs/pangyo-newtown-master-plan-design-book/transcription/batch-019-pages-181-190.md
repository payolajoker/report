### 당선작 심사 보고서

#### 심사평

- The jury statement identifies the competition goal as developing a new, innovative, publicity-worthy model for low-rise collective housing.
- The visible review names `페카 헬린(Pekka Helin)`, `야마모토 리켄(Yamamoto Riken)`, and `마크 맥(Mark Mack)` as winning entries for the B5-1, B5-2, and B5-3 blocks arranged east-west.
- The review describes the competition as well organized and the brief/site as timely, ambitious, and capable of supporting design diversity and potential.
- It notes that the submissions showed careful analysis, refined presentation methods, and attention to environmental issues.
- Remaining tasks include improving unit plans in relation to Korean traditions/lifestyles, reconsidering accessibility for physically weaker users even where terrace houses are legally treated as first-floor dwellings, and securing identity/recognizability for individual units in the Mark Mack and Pekka Helin schemes.

#### 페카 헬린 - B5-1 Block Winner

- The text says this entry creates a distinctive `장소` and uses flowing north-European curved lines to arrange housing blocks along the site's dramatic natural character.
- Jury concerns include insufficient open-space/community definition at appropriate scale and worry that the `Ribbons of Housing` may read as a very large green-roofed building.
- Ecological strengths include planting plans on roofs and terraces, but the text notes that maintaining these planted elements would require separate community rules.
- Most housing blocks face south, producing favorable view and daylight conditions, while individual unit plans require further improvement.
- The flowing arrangement is described as harmonious with existing natural surroundings, though it may require more cut-and-fill than other schemes and therefore raises ecological concerns.
- The text also calls for careful separation of vehicular and pedestrian circulation.

### 야마모토 리켄 - B5-2 Block Winner

- The jury text says the Yamamoto Riken proposal arranges units as clusters around public space, creating clearer common spaces.
- Building mass and neighborhood-cluster scale are described as well integrated with the site.
- The open spaces between housing blocks are linked to Korea National Housing Corporation's visible motto of creating a `인간적인 공동체`.
- The plan uses residential blocks around a shared deck; visible terms include `시키(Shikii)` and `마루`.
- The deck level supports flexibility, with sunken courtyards below, but the review notes concerns about outward views and market reaction to sunken gardens for ventilation/daylight below deck level.
- Caption: `▲ 야마모토 리켄(Riken Yamamoto) Riken Yamamoto & Field Shop, 일본 + (주)종합건축사사무소 건원`.

### 마크 맥 - B5-3 Block Winner

- The Mark Mack proposal is praised for minimizing damage to the existing natural setting and creating varied exterior public spaces and private gardens.
- Interior views and exterior landscape are described as carefully considered.
- The jury values the arrangement of housing blocks around private gardens/courtyards with south-facing orientation.
- Courtyard-type units are identified as the strongest of the three housing types in the scheme.
- The `ㄴ`-shaped blocks secure views and south-facing placement while making strong use of the site's advantages.
- The review raises concern about the linear residential arrangement on the northern side: it may block noise from the north but also risks cutting off green space behind the buildings.
- Caption: `▲ 마크 맥(Mark Mack) MACK firm, 미국 + (주)종합건축사사무소 동우건축`.

#### Diagrams And Captions

- `투시도`: bird's-eye view of green-roofed, curving terrace housing in a wooded slope.
- `Green Zone`: plan diagram highlighting green/forest and circulation zones.
- `Flow : 단지`: green curving bars showing the housing-block flow.

- `Flow : 바람`: fine line diagram showing wind-flow paths.
- `캐노피`: plan diagram marking canopy points in red.

#### 판교 하우징 FLOW

- The bottom green text describes `판교 하우징 FLOW` as a joint design by Helsinki-based Helin & Co and Seoul-based iArc Architects.
- Helin & Co handled housing-block and unit-plan design; iArc developed the overall housing-site concept and communication/design coordination.
- The collaboration is described as iterative, using email and telephone communication before a design meeting in Tokyo.
- The site concept developed from iArc's system diagrams into an overall district plan that accommodated Helin & Co's architectural plan.
- The design strategy is to maximize the value of the existing sloped site, treating the slope as an advantage rather than a defect.
- The text criticizes common large-scale earthwork that flattens Korean slopes and disconnects residential space from nature.

#### Concept Text Notes

- Housing blocks flow along the site slope, and gaps between blocks naturally form public spaces.
- B5-1 is described as having two public spaces with different characters: a forested hilltop community center and a lower play-yard/park.
- The hilltop community center provides an important visual identity for the complex, appearing as a wooded hill.
- Two building types are described: `테라스하우스` and `멀티패밀리하우스`.
- Terrace houses adapt to the slope in section and create private terraces.
- Multi-family houses follow a related principle but have smaller terraces.
- The text states that although the scheme starts from three basic unit types, variations produce different units; the shared trait is outward orientation with views.
- Terraces include planted edges, supporting greening at unit level and protecting privacy below.
- Author credit visible at the end: `글 : 하태석 Tesoc Hah`.
