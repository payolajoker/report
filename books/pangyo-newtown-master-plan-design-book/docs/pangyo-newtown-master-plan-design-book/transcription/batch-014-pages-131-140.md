### A8-1 Block

The page presents model photographs and a short design narrative for the A8-1 Block. Readable image captions:

- `단지 동측`: model view from the east.

Readable narrative themes:

- `자연의 유입`: introduces a green axis linked to a natural park on the northeast side of the site, with level planning and skyline control intended to respond to the formed landform and surrounding mountain scenery.
- `도시경관에 대응`: plans symbolic residential buildings responding to the urban street, differentiating building forms by urban, street-facing, and foothill zones to create a distinctive and dynamic landscape.
- `인간친화`: emphasizes a landscape-centered complex, nearby residential buildings and exterior spaces, and areas facing the living street and foothill edge; unit living spaces are differentiated by function and treated as higher-quality spaces.

Figure notes: the four large photos document massing, tower placement, internal open space, and entry sequence; the small night rendering records the intended street-edge lighting and skyline effect.

### PAN GYO HOUSING Competition: B6-1 BLOCK

Design office and team as printed:

- `(주)종합건축사사무소 건원`
- `김종국 / 함인선 / 송현석 / 유창협`

Readable design statement: the proposal begins from the purchaser's intent for housing. It frames buyers of roughly 50-pyeong terrace houses in a quiet forested hillside at the edge of Pangyo New Town as seeking something between isolated detached/country houses and apartment-complex ideas of dense community and safety. The text warns that, depending on programming, construction, and resident use, the result could become a gathering of the strengths of detached houses and apartments or merely a list of their weaknesses.

Topographic organization:

- The site is divided into three areas by slope: a steep hillside area, a gentle slope area, and a nearly flat step area.
- The steep area uses independent terrace houses as point types, forming a `리조트 빌리지`.
- The gentle slope area uses row-type terrace houses with shared cores for two households, forming a `힐사이드 빌리지`.
- The nearly flat stepped area uses area-type `이너코트(Inner-court) 빌리지`.
- Each cluster is organized as 2-4 clusters to make an appropriate neighborhood grouping, with community decks as the center of neighbor formation.
- Three villages are horizontally connected by a community axis at the same level, while vertical side paths plug into community units. The text describes identity through point, line, and plane housing types.

Concept diagram captions and labels:

- `Nature & Urban`
- `녹지 및 수체계의 보존`
- `지형의 보존`
- Visible English labels include `NATURE`, `URBAN`, `ECO RING`, `SLOPE`, `EDGE`, `STEP`, `SLOPETYPE`, `EDGE TYPE`, and `STEP TYPE`.

Figure notes: the diagrams emphasize the relationship of nature and urban fabric, preservation of green/water systems, and preservation/adaptation of slope types.
